Data refers to raw, unprocessed facts and figures without context, such as numbers, dates, and strings. Information, on the other hand, is processed data that has been organized, interpreted, and presented in a meaningful way, providing context and relevance. For example, a list of temperatures (data) becomes useful information when analyzed to show weather patterns. Data is the foundation for generating information, which aids in decision-making and understanding complex situations.
